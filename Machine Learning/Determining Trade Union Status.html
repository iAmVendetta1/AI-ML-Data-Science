Based on the above feature comparison, we can tell that generally, those who paid less in total were less likely to leave.
It also suggests that the longer someone is a member, the more they are typically willing to pay without leaving.
It is worth noting that when it comes to total spent, there is a line at which, based on how long someone has been with the union, they are more likely to leave.
However, when TotalDues is switched out for MonthlyDues, there seems to be little to no correlation in monthly costs versus the time they have spent in the union.

Decision Tree Model¶

In [30]:
dt_train_acc = []
dt_test_acc = []
depth_range = range(2,10)

# Loop through the depth range and train a Decision Tree for each depth
for d in depth_range:
    temp_tree = DecisionTreeClassifier(max_depth=d, random_state=1)
    temp_tree.fit(X_train_pca, y_train)
    dt_train_acc.append(temp_tree.score(X_train_pca, y_train))
    dt_test_acc.append(temp_tree.score(X_test_pca, y_test))

# Find the optimal max_depth based on test accuracy
dt_idx = np.argmax(dt_test_acc)
dt_opt_depth = depth_range[dt_idx]

# Print the optimal max_depth and corresponding training and validation accuracy
print(f'{"Optimal value for max_depth: ":<40} {dt_opt_depth}')

Optimal value for max_depth:             2
